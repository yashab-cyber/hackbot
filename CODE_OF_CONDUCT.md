# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **HackBot** community pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Being respectful** — Treat all community members with courtesy and professionalism
- **Being constructive** — Provide helpful feedback, share knowledge, and mentor others
- **Being collaborative** — Work together across skill levels, backgrounds, and perspectives
- **Being ethical** — Use HackBot only for authorized security testing and responsible research
- **Being accountable** — Own your mistakes, apologize when necessary, and learn from experience
- **Being inclusive** — Use welcoming and inclusive language in all interactions
- **Respecting privacy** — Do not share others' personal information without explicit consent
- **Responsible disclosure** — Report security vulnerabilities through proper channels, not public issues

### 🚫 Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment** — Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination** — Any form of exclusion based on protected characteristics
- **Sexual content** — Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind
- **Doxxing** — Publishing others' private information (addresses, emails, phone numbers) without permission
- **Promoting illegal activity** — Encouraging unauthorized hacking, distributing malware, or attacking systems without permission
- **Target sharing** — Sharing unauthorized targets, stolen credentials, or breached data in community spaces
- **Weaponization** — Using HackBot or community resources to harm individuals, organizations, or infrastructure without authorization
- **Disruption** — Sustained disruption of discussions, events, or community activities
- **Intimidation** — Threats of violence, stalking, or following (online or physical)

## 🔐 Cybersecurity Ethics

As a cybersecurity tool community, we hold ourselves to additional ethical standards:

### Required

| Principle | Expectation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Authorization** | Only test systems you have explicit written permission to test |
| **Responsible disclosure** | Report vulnerabilities to affected parties before public disclosure |
| **Data protection** | Never exfiltrate, store, or share sensitive data from targets |
| **Minimal impact** | Avoid causing damage, data loss, or service disruption during testing |
| **Legal compliance** | Comply with all applicable laws in your jurisdiction |
| **Honest reporting** | Report findings accurately without exaggeration or fabrication |

### Prohibited in Community Spaces

- 🚫 Sharing exploits targeting specific individuals or organizations without authorization
- 🚫 Requesting help attacking systems you do not own or have permission to test
- 🚫 Distributing stolen data, credentials, or personally identifiable information (PII)
- 🚫 Promoting or glorifying black-hat activities
- 🚫 Sharing 0-day exploits without coordinated disclosure with the affected vendor

## 🏛️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## 📏 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all HackBot community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki)
- Discord server
- Social media accounts and posts referencing HackBot
- Community events, meetups, and conferences
- Direct messages related to HackBot community participation

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces — for example, using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

#### 1. 📝 Correction

**Community Impact:** Minor, first-time infractions — inappropriate language, off-topic behavior, or unintentional insensitivity.

**Consequence:** A private written warning with clarification of the violation and expected behavior. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. ⚠️ Warning

**Community Impact:** A single incident or series of minor violations — repeated inappropriate behavior, disrespectful interactions, or minor ethical breaches.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the affected parties for a specified period. Violations may result in a temporary ban.

#### 3. 🔇 Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation — sustained inappropriate behavior, promoting illegal activity, sharing unauthorized targets, or a single high-impact ethical breach.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from all community interaction for a specified period (typically 7–30 days). No public or private interaction with community members during this period. Violating ban terms may result in a permanent ban.

#### 4. 🚫 Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** A pattern of serious violations, or a single egregious act — distributing malware, doxxing, sharing stolen data, unauthorized attacks on real systems, or sustained harassment.

**Consequence:** Permanent removal from all community spaces. No appeal.

## 🤝 Contribution Guidelines

When contributing to HackBot:

1. **Code review** — All contributions are reviewed for security implications before merging
2. **Responsible tooling** — New features must include appropriate safeguards (validation, rate limiting, user confirmation)
3. **Documentation** — Security-relevant features must be documented with clear usage warnings
4. **Testing** — Security-critical code must include tests
5. **No backdoors** — Contributions must not include hidden functionality, data collection, or phone-home behavior
6. **Dependency hygiene** — New dependencies should be well-maintained and audited for known vulnerabilities
